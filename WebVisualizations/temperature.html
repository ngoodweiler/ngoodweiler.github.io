Temperature vs Latitude

Maximum temperature, taken at the time of data collection (Jan 24, 2020), compared to city latitude. There is a somewhat parabolic relationship, centered roughly at 0 latitude (the equator). This relationship does make sense due to statistically higher temperatures as you get closer to the equator. The data collection was randomized, and ended up with more data points in the northern hemisphere than southern hemisphere, hence the skewed appearance on the graph. I would expect a more even curve if an equal number of cities in each hemisphere were selected. Another change to this analysis would be collecting data over an entire year, or at least multiple times per year, as the northern and southern hemispheres experience inversed seasons to each other.
